What is an Earthquake

An earthquake is a sudden shaking of the ground caused by the release of energy stored in the Earthâ€™s crust. This release of energy happens when rocks that have been under stress suddenly rupture or slip along faults. The point directly above the rupture on the surface is the **epicenter**.

Preparedness & Mitigation

Before an Earthquake

  • Check if your area is seismically active.
  • Secure heavy furniture, shelves, and appliances to the wall.
  • Prepare an **Emergency Kit** with water, non-perishable food, first-aid, and a flashlight.

During an Earthquake: Drop, Cover, and Hold On

  • **DROP** immediately onto your hands and knees.
  • **COVER** your head and neck with your arms or get under a sturdy table or desk.
  • **HOLD ON** to your cover until the shaking stops.
  • Stay away from glass, windows, and exterior walls.

After an Earthquake

  • Expect **aftershocks**.
  • Check for injuries and damage (especially gas leaks).
  • Do **NOT** enter damaged buildings.

Key Fire Response Actions

Effective fire response focuses on rapid **detection, safe evacuation, and containment**. Every second counts to save lives and limit property damage.

Emergency Procedures

Immediate Response

  • **Sound the Alarm:** Activate the nearest fire alarm and shout "FIRE!" to alert everyone.
  • **Call for Help:** Immediately call the Fire Brigade (**101 in India**) after safely evacuating.
  • **Check Doors:** Before opening a door, check for heat using the **back of your hand**. If it's hot, do not open itâ€”use an alternate route.

If Trapped by Smoke

  • **Crawl Low:** Smoke and heat rise. Crawl low to the ground where the air is cleaner and cooler.
  • **Cover Vents:** Use a cloth or towel to cover vents or cracks under the door to prevent smoke from entering.

If Your Clothes Catch Fire: Stop, Drop, and Roll

This is a critical life-saving procedure:

  • **STOP** immediately. Do not run.
  • **DROP** to the ground.
  • **ROLL** over and over to smother the flames.

Understanding Flood Risk

Flood awareness is the knowledge of how floods happen, what impacts they cause, and how to prepare and respond to them. Floods are often intensified by heavy monsoon rainfall, blocked drains, and inadequate infrastructure.

Evacuation and Safety Rules

The Danger of Water

  • **Turn Around, Don't Drown:** Never drive or walk through floodwaters. Just **six inches of moving water** can knock an adult off their feet.
  • **Avoid Wires:** Do not touch electrical equipment or wires, as floodwater can be electrified.
  • **Avoid Drinking Water:** Do not use tap water for drinking or cooking until officials confirm it is safe.

Evacuation Steps

  • **Listen to Warnings:** Follow alerts from local authorities (TV, radio, mobile apps like FloodWatch India).
  • **Move to Higher Ground:** Quickly move to the upper floors of a building or a designated elevated safe area.
  • **Prepare a Go Bag:** Have an emergency kit with food, water, medicine, and critical documents ready for a quick exit.

Cyclone & Storm Preparedness

Cyclones (Tropical Storms) bring devastating winds, torrential rainfall, and storm surges. Indiaâ€™s coastal regions, particularly the Bay of Bengal and Arabian Sea, are highly vulnerable.

Pre-Cyclone Actions

  • **Secure Structures:** Trim dead branches from trees and secure loose items outside (e.g., dustbins, tools).
  • **Stock Supplies:** Store extra drinking water, non-perishable food, and a battery-operated radio.
  • **Review Evacuation Route:** Know the quickest, safest route to your designated cyclone shelter or high-ground area.

During the Cyclone

  • **Stay Indoors:** Remain inside a safe, sturdy building, preferably in an interior room away from windows and doors.
  • **Ignore the Eye:** If the storm's center (the eye) passes, the winds may stop suddenly. This is temporary! Stay sheltered, as high winds will return from the opposite direction shortly.

Tsunami Readiness

A tsunami is a series of powerful ocean waves caused by large-scale disturbances like an underwater earthquake, volcanic eruption, or landslide. They pose a grave risk to all coastal areas.

Warning Signs & Response

Natural Warning Signs

The ocean often recedes rapidly and unusually far before the first large wave arrives. This is your most critical warning sign.

  • A strong or long **earthquake** is the primary natural warning.
  • A loud **roar** or unusual noise from the ocean.
  • The **sudden, rapid recession** of water from the beach.

Immediate Action

  • If you feel an earthquake near the coast, do not wait for an official warning. **Immediately move inland to higher ground.**
  • Tsunami waves can continue for hours. Do not return to the coast until officials declare it safe.

Landslide Safety

Landslides involve the movement of a mass of rock, debris, or earth down a slope. They are common in hilly and mountainous regions, often triggered by heavy rainfall or earthquakes.

Key Safety Measures

Warning Signs

  • Unusual sounds, like trees cracking or boulders knocking together.
  • New cracks or unusual bulges in the ground, roads, or pavements.
  • Tilting or cracking of utility poles or retaining walls.

Immediate Action

  • If you suspect an imminent landslide, **evacuate immediately**.
  • If you are caught in a moving slide, roll into a ball and protect your head.
  • Never cross a landslide-prone area at night during heavy rains.

Heat Wave Safety

A heat wave is a prolonged period of excessively hot weather, which can be dangerous, especially when accompanied by high humidity. Heatstroke and dehydration are major risks.

Staying Cool and Hydrated

Protect Yourself

  • **Stay Hydrated:** Drink plenty of water and ORS (Oral Rehydration Solution), even if you don't feel thirsty. Avoid alcohol and sugary drinks.
  • **Stay Indoors:** Limit outdoor activities, especially between 12 PM and 4 PM.
  • **Wear appropriate clothing:** Use light-colored, loose-fitting, and cotton clothes.
  • **Know the Symptoms:** Watch for signs of heat exhaustion: heavy sweating, paleness, muscle cramps, and faintness.

Epidemic & Pandemic Preparedness

An epidemic is a sudden increase in cases of a disease in a specific area, while a pandemic is an epidemic that has spread across multiple countries or continents. Preparedness focuses on hygiene and isolation.

Controlling Disease Spread

  • **Practice Excellent Hygiene:** Wash hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds.
  • **Wear Masks:** Use masks in crowded indoor settings or as advised by health officials.
  • **Maintain Distance:** Follow social distancing guidelines to limit transmission.
  • **Follow Official Advice:** Trust and follow guidelines from the World Health Organization (WHO) and local health ministries.

Chemical Hazard Response

A chemical hazard can occur due to accidental release of toxic industrial materials, requiring immediate and decisive action to avoid contamination and health risks.

Shelter-in-Place Protocol

The Primary Response

In most chemical emergencies, the safest action is to **Shelter-in-Place** (staying indoors and making the area airtight) rather than evacuating into the chemical cloud.

  • **Go Inside Immediately:** Get indoors and close all windows and doors.
  • **Shut Down Systems:** Turn off all fans, air conditioning, and heating systems.
  • **Seal Openings:** Use duct tape or plastic sheeting to seal exterior openings, cracks, and vents.

Security & Terror Attack Protocol

Security protocols focus on minimizing casualties and maximizing safety in a volatile situation. The official advice is often summarized as Run, Hide, Tell.

Run, Hide, Tell

  • **RUN:** If there is a safe and clear escape route, run away from the danger. Do not carry belongings.
  • **HIDE:** If escape is not possible, find a secure place to hide. Barricade the door if possible, silence your phone, and stay quiet.
  • **TELL (Call):** Once you are in a safe place, call the Police (**100 in India**) with precise information about the location, attackers, and weapons.

Road Safety Basics

Road accidents are a leading cause of injury. Adhering to simple safety rules as a pedestrian, cyclist, or passenger is vital.

Rules for Students

  • **Use Crosswalks:** Always use pedestrian crossings, foot overbridges, or underpasses.
  • **Look Both Ways:** Follow the **"Look Left, Look Right, Look Left Again"** rule before crossing any road.
  • **Bus Safety:** Wait for the bus to stop completely before approaching it. Never push or run near a moving bus.
  • **Wear Seatbelts/Helmets:** Always wear a seatbelt when in a car and a helmet when on a two-wheeler.

Building Collapse Response

A building collapse is a terrifying event. Survival relies on protective action and self-rescue techniques until professional help arrives.

If Trapped in Rubble

  • **Protect Yourself:** Use your arms to cover your head and mouth to protect against falling dust and debris.
  • **Signal Rescuers:** If possible, bang on a pipe or wall to alert rescuers to your location. Shout only as a last resort, as shouting can cause you to inhale dust.
  • **Stay Still:** Try to stay in one place to avoid further injury or causing more debris to fall.
  • **Cover Your Mouth:** Cover your nose and mouth with a piece of cloth to filter the air.

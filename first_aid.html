FIRST AID PROTOCOL GUIDE

CLICK ANY SECTION TO EXPAND THE STEP-BY-STEP PROCEDURE. **CALL EMERGENCY SERVICES FIRST.**

// 01. SEVERE BLEEDING (MAJOR HEMORRHAGE)

Goal: Stop the bleeding to prevent shock and blood loss.

1
DANGER, RESPONSE, SEND (DRS)

Ensure the scene is safe (D). Check for response (R). **Call emergency services (Send/911/112) immediately** or instruct someone else to call.

2
DIRECT, FIRM PRESSURE

Use a clean dressing, cloth, or even your gloved hand. Apply **direct, unyielding pressure** to the wound. **Do not peek** to see if bleeding has stopped.

3
ELEVATE AND REINFORCE

If there is no suspected fracture, **raise the injured limb above the level of the heart.** If the dressing soaks through, **do not remove it**; simply add another on top and continue pressure.

4
SECURE AND MANAGE SHOCK

Once bleeding is controlled, secure the dressing with a firm bandage. Treat for shock by lying the patient down and keeping them warm. **Continuously monitor** until help arrives.

If bleeding cannot be controlled by direct pressure, consider using a **tourniquet** (if trained and available) on the limb, placed high above the wound.

// 02. ADULT CARDIAC ARREST (CPR)

Goal: Maintain blood flow to the brain and heart until emergency services arrive.

1
CHECK & CALL (D-R-S)

Scene safe? Check for response. **Send:** Call emergency services immediately. **Airway:** Open the airway using a head-tilt/chin-lift. **Breathing:** Look, listen, and feel for normal breathing for no more than 10 seconds. If no breathing or only gasping, start CPR.

2
CHEST COMPRESSIONS (30)

Place the heel of one hand in the center of the chest, then the heel of the other hand on top. Give **30 compressions** hard and fast: at a rate of 100-120 per minute, and a depth of at least 2 inches (5 cm).

3
RESCUE BREATHS (2)

Give **2 rescue breaths**, ensuring the chest rises with each breath. Pinch the nose, maintain the head-tilt/chin-lift, and seal your mouth over theirs.

4
CONTINUE AND DE-FIB (AED)

**Continue cycles of 30 compressions and 2 breaths (30:2)** until help arrives, the person wakes up, or you are too exhausted to continue. If an **AED** arrives, use it immediately and follow its voice prompts.

If unwilling or unable to give rescue breaths, perform **Hands-Only CPR** (continuous, high-quality chest compressions).

// 03. ADULT CHOKING (SEVERE OBSTRUCTION)

Goal: Dislodge the foreign body blocking the airway.

1
ASSESS AND ENCOURAGE

Ask: "Are you choking?" If the person nods, but cannot speak, cough, or breathe, the obstruction is severe. **Call emergency services immediately.**

2
BACK BLOWS (5)

Stand to the side and slightly behind the person. Support the chest with one hand, bend them forward. Give **5 sharp blows** between the shoulder blades with the heel of your other hand.

3
ABDOMINAL THRUSTS (5)

If back blows fail, stand behind the person. Place a fist just above the navel (belly button). Grasp the fist with your other hand. Deliver **5 quick inward and upward thrusts**.

4
CONTINUE OR COLLAPSE

Continue alternating **5 back blows and 5 abdominal thrusts** until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious. If they become unconscious, immediately begin CPR (modified for an obstruction).

**For pregnant women or very large people**, use chest thrusts (same location as CPR compressions) instead of abdominal thrusts.

// 04. BURN CARE (HEAT/THERMAL)

Goal: Cool the burn to limit tissue damage and prevent infection.

1
STOP THE BURNING PROCESS

Remove the person from the source of the heat. If clothing is on fire, **Stop, Drop, and Roll.** Remove clothing or jewelry near the burn **unless it is stuck to the skin.**

2
COOL THE BURN

Run the burn under **cool or lukewarm running water** for a minimum of **20 minutes**. Do NOT use ice, ice water, or greasy substances like butter or oil.

3
COVER THE WOUND

After cooling, cover the burn with a clean, non-fluffy, non-adhesive dressing (like plastic wrap or a clean sheet). This helps prevent infection and relieve pain.

4
SEEK MEDICAL HELP

Call emergency services for large burns, burns to the face/hands/genitals, or any burn that is deep (full-thickness) or causes blistering larger than a 50p/quarter coin.

Treating a burn for the full **20 minutes of cooling** is critical and often overlooked.
